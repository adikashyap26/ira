Research and development focused on using organic media in agriculture involves exploring ways to incorporate organic materials into various agricultural practices to enhance plant growth, soil health, and overall sustainability. Organic media in agriculture often refers to substrates or growing mediums that provide a suitable environment for plant growth while reducing reliance on synthetic chemicals and promoting eco-friendly practices. IRA PLANT BIOTECH products viz., IRA PLANT-SHILAJIT, IRA PLANT PEARL AND IRA PLANT- APAS have been used in several agricultural plots for field trial. Pilot research conducted in and around Bengaluru, Karnataka resulted in healthy produce organically.

  • Organic Growing Substrates:
    Researchers are developing and optimizing organic growing substrates that are composed of natural materials such as compost, peat moss, coir (coconut fiber), and other organic matter. These substrates can be used in place of traditional soil or synthetic growing media to provide a nutrient-rich environment for plant growth.
  • Soil Health Improvement:
    Pilot studies using IRA PLANT BIOTECH products conducted in various agricultural lands was to understand how the incorporation of organic materials into soil can improve its structure, water-holding capacity, and nutrient availability. Cover cropping, composting, and mulching with organic materials contribute to enhancing soil microbial diversity and overall fertility.

The development of IRA PLANT-SHILAJIT, IRA PLANT PEARLS AND IRA PLANT- APAS, as biosupplements, and natural amendments, is an active area of research. These supplements help in release of nutrients slowly over time and improve soil health, reducing the risk of nutrient leaching and groundwater pollution.

  • Soil Carbon Sequestration:
    IRA PLANT BIOTECH products can contribute to soil carbon sequestration, which helps mitigate climate change by storing carbon dioxide in the soil. Research explores the effects of organic farming practices on increasing soil organic matter content and long-term carbon storage.
  • Biopesticides and Disease Management:
    Organic farming often relies on natural solutions for pest and disease management. Research on using IRA PLANT BIOTECH products as biopesticides and biological control agents have resulted in controlling of the pest completely and enhance biodiversity.
  • Hydroponics:
    Organic principles can be applied in hydroponic systems, where nutrient-rich solutions or organic supplements provide the basis for plant growth. Organic nutrient sources from IRA PLANT BIOTECH and pest management strategies are explored for these soilless cultivation methods.

Vertical Farming and Controlled Environment Agriculture: Integrating IRA PLANT BIOTECH materials into vertical farming and controlled environment agriculture systems allows for more sustainable and resource-efficient food production in urban areas, innovations in farming techniques were researched by IRA PLANT BITOECH COMPANY.

  • Organic Certification and Standards:
    Organic farming often relies on natural solutions for pest and disease management. Research on using IRA PLANT BIOTECH Research is conducted to establish and refine organic farming standards and certifications that ensure adherence to organic principles and practices.
  • Waste Reduction and Recycling:
    Research focuses on utilizing organic resource materials in manufacturing and packaging.
  • Community and Small-Scale Farming:
    Research addresses the implementation and use of IRA PLANT BIOTECH products among communities and small-scale farming, promoting healthy food production and sustainable livelihoods.

Collaboration between agricultural scientists, agronomists, soil scientists, ecologists, and other experts is vital to advancing research and development in using IRA PLANT BIOTECH products as media/supplements in agriculture. The goal is to create environmentally friendly and economically viable methods that promote both plant and ecosystem health while ensuring long-term food security.
